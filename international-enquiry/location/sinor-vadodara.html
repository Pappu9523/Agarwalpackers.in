Sinor is a small town in the Vadodara district of Gujarat. Situated on the banks of the Narmada, it is known as the birthplace of one of the most prolific Gujarati writers Ramanlal Vasantlal Desai. It is also believed that Sayajirao Gayakwad visited Sinor and Met Shree Lallubhai Zaverbhai Patel of Bhekhada Village. Natives in this village speak Gujarati as their first language. According to the latest census carried out, the population of this town is 11,267 people. Owing to its impeccable and beautiful infrastructure, Sinor only proudly shows its standard in India's modernization. Enriched by the historical influences of its legendary rulers, it boasts of a thriving culture full of color and life. Most of the folks living here speak either Hindi or English. It presents a harmonious blend of several religious communities like the Hindus, Muslim and Sikh residents. This town is well connected to the roots of Indian culture and heritage and, at the same time, houses Vadodara's major big industrial projects. The railway line and National route eight, which connect the municipality with the city, have Vadodara. The town is called for its abundance of Banyan (Vad) plants. Climate

Sinor is blessed with an even tropical climate throughout the year. Here the annual maximum temperature is 20°C, and the annual minimum temperature is 40°C. Little to no rainfall is observed, and July is the hottest month of the year.


How to Reach


By Road

The golden quadrilateral and its interconnected highways connect the main metropolitan cities to the country through roads. The neighboring secondary streets connect them to the rest of the cities in our country. Vadodara has a good network of interconnected roadways extending throughout the country. So, for example, one can enter Sinor from Vadodara via the Kayavarohan-Sadhali road. This is a 55 km long road and takes 1 hour and 10 minutes to cover.


By Train

The nearest railway station is the Vishwamitri railway station, connecting this city to other major cities in Vadodara. The station is located at a distance of 47-kilometers from Haldia, and this distance can be traversed in about 1 hour and 10 minutes. Passengers prefer local transport like buses, autos or cabs to reach their desired destination from the railway station.


By Air

The Vadodara airport connects this city to the rest of the country. It is at a distance of 64-kilometers. The distance can be traveled in 1 hour and 17 minutes. From the airport, passengers can take local buses, autos, or cabs to reach their destination.
