Top 10 Places You Must Visit For Weekend Getaway from Delhi

Home to more than a million people, the capital city, Delhi, was known by different names like Indraprastha, Tughlakabad and Shahjahanabad during its long history. From the narrow yet flavorful lanes of Chandani Chowk to the artistic streets of Lodhi Garden, there is nothing that you cannot find in Dilwalon ki Dilli!

Love it or hate it, nothing compares to Delhi’s charm! And it’s not just ‘in the city’, the nearby areas of this beautiful capital city are a fascination too. Whether you are fond of getting into history or visiting a religious place or simply enjoying nature, the nearby areas are the best for weekend getaways from Delhi.

So, When you are planning a break from your busy weekdays with your friends or family, check out these jewels around Delhi that you could consider exploring.

Best Places for Weekend Getaway

Top Places to Visit for Weekend Getaway are:

  • Manesar (56 km)
  • Neemrana (129 km)
  • Sariska Tiger Reserve (195 km)
  • Mandawa (232 km)
  • Lansdowne (260 km)
  • Parwanoo (265 km)
  • Pangot (310 km)
  • Sawai Madhopur (375 km)
  • Pushkar (415 km)
  • Rishikesh (241 km)

Manesar, 56 km from Delhi

Not quite far from the capital city, Manesar is a fast-growing business and industrial hub in the Gurgaon district of Haryana. The rugged, rural life of this place is something that you must witness once in a while.

Although a business hub, the areas in and around Manesar are scenic and tranquil. The Damdama Lake, the quaint little town Sohna, Sultanpur Bird Sanctuary, Manesar Golf Course, and Shree Mata Sheetla Devi Temple are some of the popular tourist attractions in Manesar.

How to Reach: Manesar can be reached from Delhi by road in about 1.5 hours via NH 48.

Best Time to Visit: November to February, as excursions here, would be uncomfortable in the scorching summer months.

Expenditure: 3K to 5K per person

Neemrana, 129 km from Delhi

Neemrana city offers hills, flora, and a royal environment to make your weekend more fantastic. A 2.5 hrs drive from Delhi, it is an old town located in Alwar, Rajasthan. It contains a few, captivating attractions that meet the demand for a unique and fulfilling experience. One of the best attractions is the historic Neemrana Fort, which has been transformed into a lavish heritage hotel where you can relax and spend your weekend with royalty and even take out adventure sports such as zip-lining.

Moreover, the Vinay Vilas Mahal is another example of a historical building that boasts beautiful architecture and royal life. It is now accessible to the public as a museum with various artefacts from bygone eras on display. Additionally, you can organise a brief getaway to this small town with family, friends, or your spouse. This town offers wonderful weather all year long, but the best time to visit Neemrana is from August to March because tourism is at its highest.

Additionally, the best city views and a trekking adventure can be found at Bala Quila. Neemrana tourism is one of the best weekend getaway from delhi because travellers can explore some panoramic areas like Siliserh Lake, which is great for nature walks, boating, and bird-watching, Other than that, historical sites like the Baori, a stepwell, and the Vrindavan Palace's ruins and remnants are fantastic places to visit for history fans. For those who want to spend their weekend with a distinctive experience, Neemrana is ideal.

Distance from Delhi to Neemrana: 129 km

Travelling time: 2 hours 50 min

How to Reach: Being on Delhi-Jaipur Highway (NH 8), it is easily accessible in just 2 hours 30 minutes’ drive from Delhi.

Best Time to Visit:Winters is the ideal time to visit Neemrana; however since it is quite green, people who are looking for some quick moments of repose can rush here any time of the year.

Expenditure: 3K to 5K per person

Sariska Tiger Reserve, 195 km from Delhi

Only 195 km away from Delhi, Sariska National Park is a famous weekend getaway tourist place near Delhi that includes grasslands, dry deciduous forests, cliffs, and rocky scenery. Moreover, The majestic Royal Bengal Tigers that roam the Reserve are well-known.

If you are a tranquil nature lover and want to take some time away from your busy life for the enjoyment of an exciting vacation, then it is one of the best places to visit.

Distance from Delhi: 195 Km

Places to Visit: Garh-Rajor Temple, Kankwari Fort, Siliserh Lake, JaiSamand Lake

Things to Do: jeep Safari, Canter Safari, Crocodile Spotting, Wildlife photography

How to Reach: Sariska is only 37 km from Alwar Railway Station, which has regular trains plying from Delhi. However, as it is well-connected via NH 8, we suggest hiring a cab/taxi for a hassle-free 5 hours journey.

Best Time to Visit: Though Sariska Tiger Reserve remains open from 1st October to 30th June, it is advisable to come here between October and March to avoid the scorching heat.

Expenditure: 4K to 5K per person (including safari charges)

Mandawa, 232 km from Delhi

A charming small town and is only 232 km away from Delhi. It is well-known for its enormously popular Mandawa Fort. The town, also known as the Open Art Gallery, is filled with exquisite Havelis and fortifications as well as paved archways. It is situated in the Shekhawati area and is dotted with magnificent houses and palaces (Havelis).

The atmosphere and energy of the location reflect the social and cultural context of the area in the past. Due to its royal appearance, it is a popular location for filming and is frequently visited by the media.

It is a significant halt for merchants and traders who traded goods along the Silk Route. The residents have a sense of appreciation for art because they were raised in a culturally rich environment and participate in artistic endeavours like painting, handicrafts, etc. Mandawa is a fantastic place to rejuvenate your mind.

Distance from Delhi: 232 Km

Places to Visit: Hanuman Prasad Goenka Haveli, Gulab Rai Ladia Haveli, Bansidhar Newatia Haveli, The Harlalka Well, Murmuria Haveli, The Chokhani Double Haveli, and Open-Air Art Gallery

Things to Do: Sightseeing, Photography, Shopping

How to Reach: The town is well connected to Delhi via NH 9; you can opt for state-run buses that ply here or hire a private taxi. Alternatively, if planning to come by train, then Jhunjhunu Railway Station (27.3 km) is the nearest to Mandawa.

Best Time to Visit: October to March

Expenditure: 2K to 4K per person

Lansdowne, 260 km from Delhi

One of the quietest hill stations in India, it is just 245 km away from the urban hush rush. Since the arrival of the British, Lansdowne has been known as one of India's most tranquil and pristine hill stations and it’s a most popular weekend getaway place you can visit.

Lansdowne resembles an antique painting because of its surrounding forests of lush oak and blue pine. The place is known for its charming churches, springs, ponds, and flowering thickets. It is the perfect getaway for vacationers because of its remote location away from the hectic crowds of the cities.

Places to visit in Lansdowne during a weekend getaway

  • Darban Singh Regimental Museum – nicely adorned, two-storeyed historical defense museum
  • Garhwali Mess – the oldest building in Lansdowne
  • the Garhwal Rifles Regimental War Memorial – a war memorial established on 11th November 1923
  • Mary’s Church – an Anglican church at the Tip N Top hill point
  • John’s Church – a Catholic Church, built in 1936
  • Bhim Pakora – two big stones well-balanced one above the other
  • Tarkeshwar Mahadev Temple – a holy abode of Lord Shiva

Unusual things to do in Lansdowne

  • Go for a boat ride at Bhulla Lake.
  • Witness picturesque views from Snow Viewpoint as well as Hawaghar.
  • Spot various migratory birds.
  • Take some life-like pictures of the imposing Himalayas coupled with stunning vistas.
  • View our customizable package to plan a trip to Lansdowne

Useful Information

  • Distance from Delhi: 287.2 km
  • Travelling time: 6 hours 30 min
  • Best Time to visit Lansdowne: Throughout the year except for monsoon

How to Reach: Lansdowne is around 6.5 hours’ drive from Delhi. Kotdwara Railway Station (40 km) is the nearest railhead to the hill station.

Best Time to Visit: March to November; avoiding the rainy season since the slopes become slippery.

Expenditure: 3K to 4K per person

Parwanoo, 265 km from Delhi

This is a location where one may take in both the activity of a city and the alluring atmosphere of a hill station. This little hamlet, which sits on the border of Himachal Pradesh and Haryana, is home to lovely apple orchards as well as mouth-watering fruit products.

Parwanoo is a 5-hour drive from Delhi, and it is the most visited place for weekend getaway near Delhi and the entire journey is worth it. The major attraction in Parwanoo is the Timber Trail. This beautiful resort is a perfect retreat, which is accessed by a cable car. The trail boasts panoramic views of the Himalayas. Apart from this, you can also relax and rejuvenate your mind, body, and soul by exploring several beautiful, green gardens when in Parwanoo.

How to Reach: Parwanoo is 35 km from Chandigarh and can be reached in around 4 hours 30 minutes’ drive from Delhi. The nearest railway station to this hill town is Kalka.

Expenditure: 5K to 6K per person

Pangot, 310 km from Delhi

Pangot is a paradise for anyone seeking quietness. A small picturesque hamlet in Uttarakhand, Pangot is known for its jungles and birds. It will take approx. 7 hours to reach Pangot from Delhi.

It has a connection to nature, and you can get a break from the grind of daily life. The drive to reach this paradise is through the forested Cheena Peak Range. Adding to the beauty of this journey, the streams passing by the area will just release all your stress.

This lovely village is a fantastic nature lover's next great adventure and an all-around breathtaking experience.

How to reach: Only 17 km from Nainital, Pangot is accessible in around 8 hours’ drive from Delhi via NH 9. The nearest railway station to the hill station is Kathgodam (52 km).

Best Time to Visit: March-June and September-November

Expenditure: 5K to 6K per person

Sawai Madhopur, 375 km from Delhi

Sawai Madhopur, the entrance to Ranthambore, is a city in Rajasthan that gained fame due to nearby Ranthambore National Park, a wildlife hotspot. It is sprawled over an area of 400 sq. km and is home to exotic flora and fauna. The city is the administrative centre for the same-named district and is very significant historically.

It is located at a distance of 350 km from Delhi, the city is known for its glorious past and several historical monuments. The city attractions give tourists a glimpse of the vibrant tradition and culture of Rajasthan.

From the Chauhans to the Mughals, many dynasties have called it home. However, it is most recognized nowadays for serving as the entrance to Ranthambore National Park.

How to Reach: Sawai Madhopur Railway Station lies on the main Delhi-Mumbai railway line. The nearest airport to the city is Jaipur International Airport (152 km). By road (via NH 48), it takes around 8 hours from Delhi to reach Madhopur.

Best Time to Visit: November to March

Expenditure: 4K to 5K per person

Pushkar , 415 km from Delhi

Pushkar is home to hundreds of magnificent temples, including the famous "Lord Brahma Temple” and a number of gurudwaras, it is a necessary pilgrimage destination for followers of the Hindu and Sikh faiths. Additionally, due to its location in the arid desert state, Pushkar experiences exceptionally warm summers, calm and pleasant winters, and mild monsoon rainfall.

Given that Pushkar is mentioned in numerous Hindu mythology and other significant Indian literature, it is thought to have been a prehistoric metropolis. Due to its unique and colourful past, this temple Town has attracted both domestic and international attention.

Distance from Delhi: 415 Km

Places to Visit: Pushkar Lake, Mahadeva Temple, Birthplace Of Meera Bai, Kishangarh, Man Mahal, Varaha Temple, Gurudwara Sahib Pushkar, Pushkar Bazaar, Rose Garden

Things to Do: Sightseeing, Shopping, Photography, Camel Ride

How to Reach: Located in the Ajmer district of Rajasthan, it is around 7 hours 30 minutes’ drive from Delhi via NH 48. The closest railway station and airport to Pushkar is in Ajmer (14 km) and Jaipur (151 km), respectively.

Best Time to Visit: While it is a year-long destination, the climate is a lot friendlier in winters. Those who want to be a part of its famous annual Camel Fair, can plan their trip in October or November.

Expenditure: 5K to 7K per person

Rishikesh, 241 km from Delhi

The yoga capital of the world, Rishikesh is the land of rishis and yogis. Located near Dehradun, it is a spiritual city in the state of Uttarakhand. The place is abuzz with travellers, who come here to learn yoga and meditation. Situated at a distance of nearly 230 km from Delhi, out of all the popular places, Rishikesh would be the best place to visit for your weekend getaway from Delhi, a perfect rejuvenating and relaxing experience for busy people.

The sacred river of India, the Ganges, flows through Rishikesh with temples built along the banks. Ganga Arti which is performed every day in the evening at the Triveni Ghat is a must-watch. Besides, Laxman Jhula and Ram Jhula attract tourists to this place a lot.

Adventurers can enjoy river rafting in the high tidal Ganges, and other activities like bungee jumping and flying fox. Staying in riverside camps is another favourite to-do for tourists visiting this amazing land.

Places to visit in Mukteshwar during a weekend getaway

  • Chauli Jaali – a spot where a Goddess fought with a demon
  • Sitla – a picturesque hill station famous for colonial style bungalows
  • Mukteshwar Inspection Bungalow – ideal place to enjoy superb sunrise views
  • Bhalu Gaad Waterfalls – the enchanting waterfalls that can be reached after trekking
  • Choco-House Chocolatiers – the only shop in the area which sells handmade centre filled European chocolates

Unusual things to do in Mukteshwar

  • Cherish striking views of the Nanda Devi Peak.
  • Enjoy a perfect escape with trekking and camping.
  • Have a bird’s eye view of the surroundings while paragliding.
  • Soak in the divine aura at the Mukteshwar Mahadev Temple.

Useful information

  • Distance from Delhi: 241 km
  • Travelling time: 8 hours 26 min
  • Best Time to visit Mukteshwar: Anytime of the year

How to Reach: Road journey from Delhi to Rishikesh takes around 5-6 hours via the NH 334. There are also multiple trains to Rishikesh starting from the Delhi Jn. Station (Old Delhi Railway Station) and New Delhi Railway Station.

Best Time to Visit: You can visit Rishikesh throughout the year. Avoid the rainy season as adventure activities like bungee jumping, etc., might be put on hold during bad weather.

Expenditure: 5K to 7K per person
