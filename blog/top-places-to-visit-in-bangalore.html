Top 10 Best Places To Visit in Bangalore in 2023

Exploring the wonderful places to visit in Bangalore!

Bangalore, also known as Bengaluru, is one of the eight megacities in India. It is also popular as the Silicon Valley of India or the IT Capital of India. Many large-scale organisations, such as ISRO, Infosys, Wipro, etc., have their headquarters in Bangalore. Therefore, the city attracts large numbers of migrants. Since it is also home to many start-ups and tech companies, it provides many employment opportunities. So, people from different parts of the country move to Bangalore for a better future.

If you are planning to relocate to Bangalore, you will surely want to know the best places to visit in Bangalore. Therefore, we made a list to help you explore the city easily.

List of Top 10 Places To Visit in Bangalore

Top 10 Places to Visit in Bangalore are:

  • Lalbagh Botanical Garden
  • Church Street
  • Cubbon Park
  • Tipu Sultan Palace
  • Jawaharlal Nehru Planetarium
  • Visvesvaraya Industrial and Technological Museum
  • Fun World Amusement Park
  • Ulsoor Lake
  • UB City Mall
  • Bangalore Palace

An overview of famous places in Bangalore

1. Lalbagh Botanical Garden

This botanical garden is one of the most popular places to visit in Bangalore. It offers a vast spread of flora and is one of the most beautiful gardens in the country. It was originally built by Haider Ali in 1760 and was later modified by Tipu Sultan. Spread across 240 acres of land; it has over 1800 species and varieties of plants and herbs. Therefore, it is renowned worldwide for its rare species of plants and trees. Some of the plants here are of Afghani, French, and Persian origin.

Furthermore, the 3000 million-year-old Lalbagh Rock is also found here and attracts many tourists. Additionally, this huge garden is also home to a Crystal Palace-inspired glass house where flower shows are held annually. Moreover, you can also spot several birds, such as Parakeets, Brahminy Kites, Common Egrets, Mynas, etc. This place is a favourite of photographers and a treat for the eyes. It also has an in-house aquarium. So, if you visit this place, you get to see the best and rare foliage, birds, and fishes. It is an experience you shouldn't miss.

Location: Mavalli, Bangalore

Timings: Monday-Sunday, 6 AM to 7 PM

Related Read: Malls in Bangalore

2. Church Street

This 750 mt long shopping street is one of those places to visit in Bangalore that you should not miss! It is one of the busiest streets in the city. Lined with fashion shops, gift shops, bookstores, restaurants, and cafes, this place has many things to offer. You can find great deals and good quality at low prices. Some of the shops on this street are almost a hundred years old.

Furthermore, on some days, local artists set up shops on this street. You can buy their artwork, such as paintings, posters, etc., at reasonable prices. This place is also home to several arcades for children and tattoo parlours for adults. The animation souks on this street are a favourite of pop culture fans. You can find all sorts of quirky T-shirts and movie merchandise here.

Location: Church Street, Bangalore

Highlights: Cafes, restaurants, rooster guitars, bookstores, arcades, etc

3. Cubbon Park

Cubbon Park is one of the must-visit places in Bangalore. The park is named after Lord Cubbon, who laid the first foundation for this place. It is a favourite spot for nature lovers and family outings. The second largest aquarium in India, the Bangalore Aquarium, is also situated here. Spread over 300 acres, the park is home to nearly 6000 trees. Hence, it supports the ecosystem.

Apart from the beautiful natural sights, you can also take in the architectural beauty of this park. Toy train rides for children take you through the park. Statues of prominent personalities can be found at various places here. Other major tourist attractions are Lotus Pond, Ringwood Circle, and the Dancing Fountain.

Location: Kasturba Road, Bangalore

Timings: Open all days

4. Tipu Sultan Palace

The summer palace of the ruler of Mysore is one of the places to visit in Bangalore you can't miss. Adorned by the name Rashk-e-Jannat which means Envy of Heaven, it was the home of Tipu Sultan during the summers. Tipu Sultan's liking for art and culture can be seen in this palace which is a perfect blend of Indo-Islamic architecture. The sturdy columns, ornamental frescoes, motifs, and huge balconies offer a peek into the royal life of ancient times. The lush gardens and magnificent fountains attract a lot of tourists. A section of the Fort was recently converted into a museum. This place gives the feeling that you have stepped into an old era.

Location: Albert Victor Road, Bangalore

Timings: Open on all days, 8.30 AM to 5.00 PM

5. Jawaharlal Nehru Planetarium

This is a place loved and visited by kids, adults, and older adults. It is administered by BASE, which stands for Bangalore Association for Science Education. This planetarium is a treat for all science enthusiasts. You can find explanations of almost all scientific aspects, from how planets are formed to how gravity works. The Sky Theatre is the star of this place. However, that is not the only attraction. Kids and teachers love this place because it imparts scientific knowledge in an easily understandable, fun manner.

Location: Sankey Rd, Bengaluru

Timings: 10:00 AM – 5:30 PM

6. Visvesvaraya Industrial and Technological Museum

This museum is another one of the places to visit in Bangalore. Even if you are not a science enthusiast or a knowledge seeker, this place is bound to keep you hooked. Built to honour Sir Visvesvaraya, a Bharat Ratna recipient, this place takes up an area of 43000 square feet. It comprises four floors. Each floor is dedicated to a different discipline of science. The steam engine and aeroplane exhibits complement the decor inspired by scientific tools and machinery. This museum also has a planetarium, a gaming zone, and a 3D centre for recreation. Daily interactive demonstrations and experiments are conducted here.

Location: Kasturba Road, Bangalore

Timings: 10 AM to 6 PM

Related Read: Engineering Colleges In Bangalore

7. Fun World Amusement Park

This amusement park is one of the most popular places to visit in Bangalore. Spread over 22 acres of land, it is home to many recreational rides and water rides as well. Ferris wheels, bumper cars, and roller coasters are the main attractions. It also features a food court area and a huge garden to rest. There are rides to suit everybody. In total, it has 50 rides, among which some are for kids, and others are rides for adults and adrenaline rides. Whether you visit this place for recreation or to seek a thrill, you will not be left unsatisfied.

Location: J.C Nagar, Bangalore

Timings: 11:00 AM - 7:00 PM

8. Ulsoor Lake

While many cities have lakes on the outskirts, Ulsoor Lake is located in the heart of Bangalore city. Spread over 125 acres, this place offers a calm and scenic spot for locals and tourists. You can sit back, relax, and watch the endemic birds fly in and out. The most popular activity at Ulsoor lake is boating. You can either take a pedal boat or a rowboat to enjoy the lake.

You can even stop at points to admire the lake from different viewpoints. People often come here for morning and evening walks on the tracks around the lake. Many eateries around offer different types of street food. This place is also called Halasuru Lake.

Location: Ulsoor Lake, Bangalore

Timings: 6:00 AM – 8:00 PM; Closed on Wednesdays

9. UB City Mall

This mall is spread over 13 acres of land in the Central Business District. This mall is one of the places to visit in Bangalore if you want to shop for high-end brands. If luxury shopping is what you are looking for, UB City Mall is the place to be. Apart from this, you also have various fine dining options that are some of the finest in the city.

Furthermore, this mall has entertainment and recreational areas available too. Comprising four towers, UB City Mall is a sight to marvel at. It also features a high-end spa where you can unwind after a tiring day. The nightlife at UB City mall is alive with Karaoke, parties, and DJs playing electrifying music.

Location: Central Business District, Bangalore

Timings: 11:00 AM - 11:30 PM

10. Bangalore Palace

The Bangalore palace is the last on this list of places to visit in Bangalore. This majestic palace is full of royal architecture and beauty. Made in 1878, this magnificent palace spreads across 45,000 square feet. Its wooden structure and regal carvings lend it a majestic air. This palace is said to be inspired by Windsor Castle in London. The huge ballroom and the Durbar room are the main attractions here. Furthermore, this palace is also home to several cultural events, high-profile marriages, and rock show events.

Location: Vasanth Nagar, Bangalore

Timings: 10:00 AM - 5:30 PM
