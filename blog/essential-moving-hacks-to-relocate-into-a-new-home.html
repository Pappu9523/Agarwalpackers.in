15 Essential Moving Hacks to Relocate into a New Location Easily

Moving to a new location can be thrilling, but the packing and moving process is arduous for many. Packing everything on your own and taking care of delicate items while shifting is undoubtedly a daunting task. Choosing the right boxes for packing can also be difficult if you are doing it for the first time.

Avoiding common moving blunders and making advance plans for a smoother transition is crucial to a successful move. Develop a plan to save your time, work, and energy. The best idea would be to hire a professional service provider like Agarwal Packers and Movers Bade Bhaiya. They will undertake the entire process and ensure a smooth house shifting. But if you still want to do everything on your own, we have listed 15 important things that will help you. Continue reading to know them:

Before You Get Started

It is a golden rule to plan things before starting. Be aware of the route, and have an idea in mind about your things. Once your location is decided and you are prepared for the relocation, the following steps are going to be helpful.

15 Packing and Moving Hacks to Make Relocation Easier

Use these 15 moving and packing hacks to make the relocation process and make it less stressful:

1. Plan things ahead

As already discussed, proper planning can help you reduce your stress and ensure you get little to no annoyance during the entire process. It is also important to choose the medium of transportation. You can get all the answers based on your needs with a good plan in position. Having boxes for packing and a to-do list benefits tremendously. It will make things go smoothly.

2. Set a budget

Considering the expenses regarding packing and moving, relocation can be costly. It is advisable to set a budget. If you have a reasonable moving budget, your relocation won't cost more than you can pay. It depends on your budget if you can afford an all-inclusive or DIY move.

Read Also: IBA Approved Packers and Movers

3. Declutter

It is exhausting to pack all your belongings in boxes and other containers. By reducing clutter, you can make life somewhat simpler for yourself. Go through everything you own, donating or discarding everything you don't need any longer. You can pack faster using this method, and the moving truck will have more room for you. You'll have less to unpack as well.

4. Get supplies

Buy packing materials and moving boxes. Box cutters, markers, bandages, tape, paper towels, and trash bags can get ordered or bought. One of the last things you want to happen on packing day is to run out of materials. It's essential to purchase more boxes for packing than you intend to use. Renting moving equipment from a company is the best option for moving heavier items.

5. Packing in boxes

It is best to begin packing as early as possible. Books and other hefty goods should be packed in compact boxes while moving. Larger ones may hold lighter stuff like cushions and linens. Large boxes that are filled with heavy objects not only complicate moving tasks but also have a significant risk of breaking. Closing the bottom and top seams with a few pieces of tape, then using one of the moving professionals' tricks by creating a few wraps around the box's top and bottom corners, where there is tension.

6. Avoid empty spaces

You can use packing paper, clothes, or towels to fill empty spaces in the packaging box. Use your sheets and towels as cushioning to cut costs on packing supplies. There will be a need for a lot of bubble wrap. Use it to save breakables like plates, frames, lamps, and more with your towels and linens.

7. Label everything

As you pack the boxes, label them. When you arrive at your new house, you should be able to recognise each box's proper location right away. To make it simple to locate the piece that you require in a stack, try marking each side with a marker. Additionally, keeping a list of inventories and numbering them is an excellent method to keep a record of your package.

Read Also: Tips for a Green and Safe Relocation

8. Keep the essentials and valuables with you

Place your daily necessities in a bag or case you will carry. You will at least have the essentials with you even if things don't go as per plan. On the day of the move, identify a secure location to keep any valuables you may have. Make a fast backup of your vital files before moving a computer.

9. Create a moving folder

Gather addresses, rental paperwork, contracts, and other information in a single folder. You will have the documents, payments, and more if any inquiries arise during the planning stage or the move. If your phone or computer battery dies while you're moving, think about using a physical copy rather than a digital one.

10. Take photos

To keep yourself in the loop of setting up your entertainment systems after moving them, take pictures of the intricate wiring on the back of the units. In case you have hired someone, any fragile or priceless items should be photographed just in case they disappear or get harmed during transport. When you need to file a moving insurance claim, the pictures will come in very handy.

11. Moving kitchen items

Always pack dishes on their sides and never flat. Use a cardboard packaging box with partitions to help preserve the glasses while packing them for a move, and wrap them in many layers of paper to further protect them. Bowls and cups fit inside of one another. Place a lot of crumpled-up paper above and below as padding.

12. Wardrobe materials and TV

You can place folded clothing in bags, cardboard boxes, or a dresser. Clothing will be simpler to transport and arrange in your new house if you leave them on hangers. Safeguard your shoes from one another when packing them for a move. Wrap each shoe separately to prevent buckles or sharp heels from causing harm to other shoes and to keep dirt away from one another. Shrink-wrap the dresser to protect it. The plastic prevents the drawers from disengaging during transport.

If you are packing yourself, place the box holding the TV inside another box cushioned with packing paper. TVs risk getting damaged if they get placed flat if they are not in the original packaging box. Some movers will box TVs just like furniture if you hire them to do the job. But specific wooden crates are needed for plasma TVs.

13. Schedule utilities

Stay in touch with your utility suppliers to arrange for services at your new home. You don't want to go without the necessities when you get there. Plan in prior, and document your requests.

14. Loading ramp

A vehicle with a ramp is a vital requirement if you are a do-it-yourself mover. Although renting a truck without one might be less expensive, a loading ramp-equipped truck is preferable. You will avoid tirelessly lifting each box and furniture into the vehicle. This action will take longer and result in sore muscles.

15. Decide on a moving company

You may decide if you can manage it yourself or need to employ professional movers once you have your budget in position. Start by asking friends and family for recommendations and looking into the best moving firms. Before making a choice, compare quotations from several businesses. In addition, waiting until the last moment is a bad idea. So, to avoid missing anything, be ready with a moving checklist.

Things that Shouldn't Get Packed in Moving Boxes

When moving, you will get the temptation to pack everything. However, there are a few things that can be left behind, such as

  • Food in open packets.
  • Documents (personal identification, wills, medical records, financial documents, etc.)
  • Valuable items (collectibles, jewellery, cash)
  • Gardening supplies (pesticides, etc.)
  • Inflammable (paint, batteries, solvents, and chemicals)

There is no one-size-fits-all manual since there are many specifics and moving pieces. Things differ for different people as they will have distinct needs and worries.
