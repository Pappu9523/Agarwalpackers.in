Auspicious Dates for Griha Pravesh Muhurat 2023 to Shift into a New Home

When shifting to a new house, people often decide to hold a House warming ceremony according to the best Griha Pravesh Muhurat 2023. Such a ceremony ensures that your stay in your new home brings prosperity and happiness, thereby removing the negativity. In India, we call it the Griha Pravesh Pooja. It is a housewarming puja that brings positive vibes and bestows good fortune to those who live there.

Furthermore, as per the Hindu traditions and rituals, the Griha Pravesh Pooja or ceremony is done on an auspicious day, according to the New Home Vastu. The benefit of such a gruhapravesam ceremony or Puja is that people get the blessings of good health, prosperity, peace and happiness. In addition, when you have purchased a new house and are ready to move, the Griha Pravesh Pooja ensures a smooth process. Here we consider the 'Shubh Muhurat' or 'Griha Pravesh Muhurat' based on which we pick the auspicious date.

Kinds of Griha Pravesh

This section looks at the significance of each kind of Griha Pravesh ceremony. One can perform this Puja for a new house and after renovating the old one. Secondly, some people resettling in their old homes also prefer going for a Griha Pravesh ceremony to keep the negativity and evils at bay.

Apurva Griha Pravesh for a New Home

This kind of Griha Pravesh ceremony is performed when a person shifts to a new house with family. Here, the house can be new construction or an old house bought by the person. However, people primarily follow the Apurva Griha Pravesh ceremony when purchasing a newly constructed home.

Sapurva Griha Pravesh for Re-entry into an Old Home

People conduct this ceremony on re-entering an old home on an auspicious muhurat. Such a ceremony happens when the person or a family comes to their native home after a long time. In another situation, one can perform the Sapurva Griha Pravesh ceremony when shifting to old homes from temporary accommodation.

Dwandwa Griha Pravesh for Renovated Homes

People may move to their old homes when constructing a new house. Here, they can perform the Dwandwa Griha Pravesh ceremony on completing the renovation. It helps drive away all the negativity from home. People may renovate or reconstruct their houses when there is a significant change. Secondly, they may want to be safe while residing there if the house has taken much damage due to natural calamities such as earthquakes, floods, etc.

How to Perform Griha Pravesh Pooja?

People perform the Griha Pravesh ceremony or Puja at their new or renovated homes before finally home shifting. It eliminates negativity and bad omen, replacing them with good health, prosperity, and happiness. Talking about performing the Griha Pravesh puja, it has the most common rituals - New Home Vastu Puja and Hawan. Keeping these rituals in mind, the person and family ensure blessings of peace and harmony for their new home.

Checkout the Steps to Perform the Griha Pravesh Pooja in 2023

To perform the Griha Pravesh Pooja, we must follow the necessary steps. Here we take a look at the steps to make the tradition successful.

Considering an Auspicious Date for Griha Pravesh

Talking about the Hindu tradition, a person must first enter a home on an auspicious date. Such data is picked based on the Hindu calendar, also called Panchang. The belief says that good luck and prosperity come to a person within the four months as per the Lunar calendar. They are called Magha, Phalguna, Baisakh and Jyeshta. Make sure you consider the auspicious date within these months.

Decoration matters

People often decorate their homes with fresh marigold flowers and mango leaves whenever a ritual takes place, according to Hindu traditions. Such a decoration is considered auspicious and thus brings positivity to the new home. Meanwhile, the ladies in the family decorate their house with Rangoli. It further attracts the divine energy to bring joy to the family.

Performing New Home Vastu Puja

Before stepping into the new house, a priest recites several mantras. These mantras help seek blessings for a healthier and happier life for the people of that house. People also follow the ritual of breaking an ash gourd and coconut. The head of the family performs it and leaves them in front of the house.

Performing Kalash Puja

To perform Kalash Puja, you need to take a copper pot. Fill it with water, different grains, and a coin. Then, a coconut wrapped with red cloth is placed on top of the copper pot or Kalash with mango leaves and coconut peel. As the priest recites the mantras, the head of the family (man and woman) carries this copper pot inside the house. They have to step inside the house by putting their right foot first. After that, the copper pot or Kalash is kept near the Havana area.

Performing Havana Shanti

It is a fire ritual performed according to Hindu tradition to eliminate the sins committed during new house construction, namely digging the earth, stone breaking, and tree cutting. Furthermore, people often worship Lord Ganesha during Havana Shanti. It helps in purifying the house as well as removing negativity. In addition, Goddess Lakshmi is also worshiped to keep financial prosperity intact. Havana Shanti includes offerings such as turmeric, milk, ghee, etc., to the sacred fire.

Boiling the milk

When performing the Havana Shanti ceremony, the lady in the family takes responsibility for boiling milk. She would let it spill to signify that there should always be an abundance and prosperity in the family.

Offering a meal to the priest

After performing the Griha Pravesh ceremony, offering a meal to the priests and others is a ritual.

Presence of others

On the auspicious day of Griha Pravesh Puja, the people who have come into the new house are offered to stay overnight. Secondly, the house owners make sure to keep the oil lamp burning. It signifies that divine energy and positivity remain inside the house forever.

Griha Pravesh Puja Do's and Don'ts

Things to keep in mind while doing your House Warming Ceremony. Keeping the do's and don'ts in mind to ensure that the house warming ceremony goes well.

  • Based on the Griha Pravesh Muhurat, the Griha Pravesh Puja should be performed when the new house construction is complete. Nothing should be temporarily placed in the house.
  • During the gruhapravesam pooja, breaking the coconut by the head of the family is a must. You should do it at the threshold of the new house before entering the new home.
  • Make sure to put your right foot on the first step inside the new house. That signifies prosperity and good health.
  • The period of Holashtak starts a week before the Holi festival. However, one must ensure not to move into the new house during this period.
  • People who want to shift to their new house in December make sure to perform the housewarming ceremony on the days that fall in the Lunar month. Margashirsha month is auspicious; meanwhile, the month of Pausch is not recommended because it might bring bad fortune.
  • If any of the ladies are carrying a baby or are pregnant, make sure not to perform the Griha Pravesh ceremony for the new house.
  • As per Hindu tradition, the male members of the family should step into the new house with their right leg first. In contrast, the female members step with their left leg first.
  • You should use fresh flowers and mango leaves when decorating the new house.
  • Griha Pravesh pooja is for the entire family members for the blessings and prosperity in the new house. One should not leave the new house vacant during the ceremony.
  • Don't forget to sprinkle holy water on the mango leaves at every corner of the new house.
  • Don't forget to give Dakshina to the Brahmins present at the new house. Also, make sure to provide something to the poor. Please don't keep them hungry.
  • If you are looking forward to bringing the household items, furniture, etc., into the new house, ensure it is done after completing the Griha Pravesh Puja. However, you can use a gas stove indoors for boiling milk, preparing prasad, etc.
  • Do keep the idols of Gods/Goddess facing the east direction of the new house.
  • If a death of a close relative occurred recently, it is advisable not to perform the Griha Pravesh ceremony.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is done on Griha Pravesh?

Griha Pravesh Pooja can be basic or elaborate depending on the owner of the house. Normally, a havan is conducted to eliminate negative energy from the new house. A Ganesha Puja, Navagraha Shanti, and that implies the love of the nine planets, and a Vastu Puja, are for the most part performed.

Which month is not good for Griha Pravesh?

There are no Griha Pravesh dates in the long stretch of Mid-July to November 2023. Shravan, Bhadrapad, Ashwin, and Kartik months are not viewed as really great for any sure movement like the Griha Pravesh Ceremony. It is accepted that playing out a Puja during these months brings monetary misfortune, medical problems, and negative energy to your home.

What is the best day to move into a new house?

Move into your new home on a Thursday, believed by some, to be the most fortunate day. That is, except if every other person is continuing on a Thursday, which will make getting a van quite troublesome.

Why do we boil milk in our new house?

Hindu custom makes them Boil milk in your new home in an open pot until it boils over the sides. This represents the wealth of flourishing and food which will favor the new home. After it has boiled over and you offer some to your preferred divinities, serve the milk with a touch of sugar to the visitors in your new home.
