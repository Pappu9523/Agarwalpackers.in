Because of this, people from all over the world, especially India ( and other Asian countries)— move to Dubai. Moving to Dubai: Quick Reference

Dubai's laws and regulations are quite stringent. You should understand the laws and norms of the land to enjoy a good life there. Your ambition of relocating to Dubai could become a huge mess if your profile is susceptible.

If you are planning an international shifting and establishing yourself in Dubai, here is a fast reference guide for Indians. The article is to guide your carefree shifting to the dream city.

1. Get a Valid Visa

You cannot relocate to Dubai without having a valid visa. The first step in your relocation process should be obtaining a legal visa if you intend to settle in Dubai from India.

There are numerous alternatives for visas, including

  • Student Visa
  • Employment Visa
  • Investor Visa
  • Dependent Visa
  • Property-owned Visa

2. Find a place to live

The first and most important thing is to find a home. You can rent a house initially. However, if you have enough money, you can also buy a new house. In that regard, thorough research is recommended. Find a suitable location to buy or rent a house.

Here are some of the common moving places in Dubai are:

  • Dubai Marina is among the most highly rated residential areas in Dubai. The Dubai Marina Mall and a variety of eateries are located there.
  • Bur Dubai is home to a Hindu temple and a reputable Indian high school.
  • Dubai Investment Park: This area is popular for residential, commercial, and industrial purposes.

The best part about owning a property is that you can rent it out if you need to leave Dubai at any time. Also, renting your property can act as a valuable source of income in the future.

Below are the top 6 factors that you must consider while relocating to Dubai from India:

  • Get Yourself a Secure Job
  • Explore the cuisine and way of life
  • Look for Medical Facilities
  • Travel Tourist Destinations
  • Enjoy the nightlife in Dubai
  • Honour Local People and their Culture

1. Get Yourself a Secure Job

Dubai is exorbitant. It will be challenging to go days without work here unless you have adequate savings. Most Indians work locally in India before obtaining their employment visa to go to Dubai. If it's not possible, apply for a visit visa and work in Dubai. However, you would have less time in the latter scenario; the first path is more considerable.

Begin your job search as early as possible by creating a professional CV. Determine your career field, and look for opportunities in that sector. Additionally, keep an eye on the other open general positions so they might serve as a fallback for you.

2. Enroll your kids in a school or a university.

The next important job after moving to Dubai with your family is to look for a recognised school or university where you can enroll your kids. There are many reputable schools available in Dubai. In actuality, there are numerous Indian schools here that offer ICSE and CBSE curriculum. It would help if you started looking into schools well in advance to enroll your child in the right time frame and with the best schools.

3. Explore the cuisine and way of life

In terms of food, there is a lot to discover. Besides Dubai’s native food, you can enjoy other cuisines too. There are many Indian restaurants where you can enjoy authentic Indian food. All of the local spices and ingredients are available in the supermarkets there. On the whole, you will not struggle in Dubai regarding food.

4. Look for Medical Facilities

In Dubai, employers are required to offer their employees medical insurance. The medical infrastructure in Dubai is also extremely good. In actuality, there has been a lot of development in recent years.

5. Travel Tourist Destinations

For travelers, Dubai has many attractions. It would help if you visited all of the main tourist attractions there. Visiting the top destinations will start rooting your love for the place. You can visit multiple destinations, from the Burj Khalifa to desert safaris, theme parks, beaches, and architecture.

6. Enjoy the nightlife in Dubai

You are in the perfect place if you enjoy throwing parties. Dubai has everything you need. You have lots of alternatives for dining, drinking, and dancing. To avoid violating the law, make sure only to consume alcohol at Dubai's authorized establishments. You are advised to take a taxi home straight from the club or bring your car to prevent getting into difficulties or being spotted intoxicated in public. As proof that you are above 21, always have your ID on you.

In terms of Dubai's pubs and clubs, the dress code is primarily formal and polished. Therefore, when you visit one, avoid wearing jeans and try to dress up formally. Ladies are free to don their dressings, but it's best to pack a jacket or shawl to cover up with when it's cold outside.

7. Honour Local People and their Culture

Since you are an outsider trying to settle in Dubai, you must gel up with the local culture. They have a tolerant form of Sharia law, and Islam is the predominant religion there. The friendly townspeople openly welcome people of other cultures. Therefore, they, too, merit from you the same respect and kindness.

Conclusion

By the conclusion of this information, life in Dubai is pretty comfortable. If you've been given an opportunity to move to Dubai, it would be wise to think about relocating there to enjoy the tax-free income and higher standard of living that is comparable to that found in some of the best cities on earth.

Frequently Asked Questions

Will I Need to Learn Arabic to Live in Dubai?

If you are willing to move to Dubai, it is not essential to learn Arabic because English is the second language which is mostly preferred by the people in Dubai. Apart from that, almost all businesses prefer English as a first language around the workplace. So if you are moving to Dubai then you should have to be aware of some general phrases of Arabic language only.

Are There Any Restrictions Related To Covid-19 When Moving To Dubai From India?

When you are moving to Dubai during a COVID-19 pandemic, you will have to face the requirement of COVID-19 testing:

  • You have to be submitted a negative COVID‑19 PCR test certificate (test should be conducted within 48 Hours)
  • Rapid PCR test report conducted at the departure airport within 6 hours
  • At the airport, another Covid-19 test will be administered

What Else Should I Know Before Moving From India To Dubai?

You should consider the following tips before moving to Dubai from India -

  • Need to convert your INR currency into Emirati dirham before Moving to Dubai because the cost of living in Dubai is higher than India.
  • It is essential to have a medical insurance
  • You must possess a license If you plan to transport or consume alcohol in Dubai, regardless of whether you're drinking at home or at a friend's place. You will want copies of your passport, visa, passport photo, and Emirates ID in order to obtain a license.
