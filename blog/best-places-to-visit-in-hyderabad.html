Top 10+ Best Places to Visit in Hyderabad in 2023

Hyderabad, also called Pearl City, is an amalgamation of India's modernity and cultural history, captivating visitors each year. Hyderabad has a history of 400 years and promises a fantastic experience irrespective of the time of your visit. It has natural getaways, historical monuments, delicious cuisines, and enthralling entertainment parks and shopping places.

The top places to visit in Hyderabad are amusing for all age groups, from young champs to old folks. You get to see two faces of this place- New Hyderabad and Old City. The former has modern developments, and the latter speaks about the city's cultural heritage.

Best Places to Visit in Hyderabad

Top 10 Places to Visit in Hyderabad are:

  • Golconda Fort
  • Charminar
  • Ramoji Film City
  • Chowmahalla Palace
  • Birla Mandir
  • Jala Vihar
  • Qutub Shahi Tombs
  • Lumbini Park
  • Birla Planetarium
  • Salar Jung Museum
  • Tank Bund
  • Purani Haveli
  • Durgam Cheruvu
  • Chilkur Balaji Temple
  • Taj Falaknuma Palace

Briefing of Hyderabad Famous Places

Golconda Fort

Away from the city is the Golconda fort, built by the Qutub Shahi Kings, with 87 bastions and eight gates. Its majestic walls are as tall as 15 to 18 feet, with a perimeter of approximately 11 km. Its brilliant architecture is paired with amazing acoustics, which adds to the charm of the fort. It also has an astounding water supply system, with Ramdas' prison and "Rahban" cannon.

The fort is open every day of the year for visitors between 09:00 am to 05:30 am. It also has a sound and light show divided between the executive and normal classes, which also differs from the fee for both classes.

Address: Khair Complex, Ibrahim Bagh, Hyderabad, Telangana 500008

Timings: 9:00 AM - 5:30 PM

Cost: Adult Entry Fee: 15.00 per person | Child Entry Fee: NA | Foreign Nationals Fee: 200 per person

Charminar

Hyderabad, the capital of Telangana, has always attracted many tourists and has a beautiful contrast of culture and age-old traditions in its amenities and modern facilities of a city. In the old city is the famous Charminar, constructed in 1591 by Sultan Mohammed Quli Qutb Shah.

In each corner, the Charminar has four towers inspired by which it is also referred to as the "Arc de Triomphe of the East." On each side, the minarets have a height of 48.7 m, with four storeys each. On the top floor, there is also a mosque on top of the Charminar. There are specifically 45 prayer spaces that attract a lot of visitors.

Address: Charminar Rd, Char Kaman, Ghansi Bazaar, Hyderabad, Telangana 500002

Timings: 9:30 AM - 5:30 PM

Cost: Adult Entry Fee: 5.00 per person | Child Entry Fee: 5.00 per person | Foreign Nationals Fee: 100 per person

Ramoji Film City

Since 1991, Ramoji Film City has attracted many visitors and is one of the biggest film studios in the world. It can house approximately 50 film units, and for that, it holds a place in the Guinness Book of World Records.

Its various sets, such as the Hollywood sign, London Street, Japanese gardens, etc., attract visitors. Its good technical facilities and architecture attract filmmakers regarding the post and pre-production of their films.

Address: Ramoji Film City Main Rd, Hyderabad, Telangana 501512

Timings: 9:00 AM - 5:30 PM

Cost: Adult Entry Fee: 1250.00 per person | Child Entry Fee: 1050.00 per person | Foreign Nationals Fee: 1250 per person

Chowmahalla Palace

The Chowmahalla palace refers to four palaces. It was built in the 18th century on more than 45 acres of land, but now only covers 12 acres of land. It has two courtyards- the southern courtyard and the northern courtyard.

The southern courtyard has four palaces- Aftab Mahal, Mahtab Mahal, Taniyat Mahal, and Afzal Mahal. Since 2005, the palace has been open for visitors, and some of its highlights include Roshan Bungalow, Council Hall, Clock Tower, and Khilwat Mubarak.

Address: 20-4-236, Motigalli, Khilwat, Hyderabad, Telangana 500002

Timings: 10:00 AM - 5:00 PM

Cost: Adult Entry Fee: 50.00 per person | Child Entry Fee: 10.00 per person | Foreign Nationals Fee: 200 per person

Birla Mandir

Located on the hill of Kalapahad, the name of the Birla Mandir had been derived from the industrialist Birlas, who constructed similar temples in several states of the country. Made in 1976, it took approximately 2000 tonnes of marble from Rajasthan to build this stunning architecture.

The Birla Mandir has an amazing 11 feet tall statue of Lord Lakshminarayana with a humongous lotus circulating it. It has many other shrines for deities such as Andal, Padmavati, and Venkateswara. It also has a statue of Lord Buddha. The Mandir is known for its architectural designs inspired by Rajasthani architecture, Utkal temple architecture, and South Indian architecture.

Address: Birla Mandir Address: Hill Fort Rd, Hyderabad, Telangana, 500004, India

Timings: 4:30 AM to 1:30 PM | 2:30 PM to 9:00 PM

Cost: Free

Jala Vihar

One known place in Hyderabad is Jala Vihar. It is an entertainment park suitable for adults and kids, offering various activities such as river rides, mushroom umbrellas, tilt buckets, mini aqua trailers, family slides, pendulums, and float slides.

Many other amusing activities include battery bikes, super-jet, mini-train, mini coarsely, mini rainbow wheel, battery cars, water shooters, and air hockey. It also has an aesthetic vedhika and amphitheater. The entry fee of Jala Vihar fluctuates for an adult and a child, but presently for kids below three feet of height, the entry fee is ₹ 250 per person, whereas, for an adult, it is ₹ 350.

Address: Necklace Road, 22/9, PV Narasimha Rao Marg, Hyderabad, Telangana 500063

Timings: 10:30 AM - 7 PM

Cost: Adult Entry Fee: 400.00 per person | Child Entry Fee: 300.00 per person

Qutub Shahi Tombs

Among some of the oldest heritage sites in Hyderabad, the Tombs are of the 7 Qutub Shahi rulers. They are located 850m away from Golconda Fort's Banjara Darwaza. It has a mixture of Indian and Persian architecture. In the 19th century, it was renovated by Mir Yousuf Ali Khan and Salar Jung III.

The dome-shaped roofs of the Qutub Shahi Tombs had been constructed with grey granite and had several beautiful designs made on them. Its garden is a popular picnic spot known as "Ibrahim Bagh."

Address: Qutub Shahi Tombs, Hyderabad, Telangana 500008

Timings: 9:30 AM – 6:30 PM

Cost: Adult Entry Fee: 10.00 per person | Child Entry Fee: 5.00 per person | Foreign Nationals Fee: 140 per person

Lumbini Park

Made by the Hyderabad Urban Development Authority in 1994, the name of Lumbini park was inspired by Lord Buddha's birthplace. It is one of the top places to visit in Hyderabad and is situated near Hussain Sagar Lake. A giant clock is one of the most attractive things at the park entrance. This clock has been designed with various flowering plants that resonate with nature's beauty.

Address: Opposite Secretariat New Gate, Hussain Sagar, Khairtabad, Hyderabad, Telangana 500004

Timings: 9 Am – 9 PM

Cost: Adult Entry Fee: 20.00 per person, Child Entry Fee: 10.00 per person, Foreign Nationals Fee: 100 per person

Birla Planetarium

Located at Naubat Pahad at the heart of Hyderabad is Birla Planetarium, inaugurated by the late Sri N.T. Rama Rao. When developing the Planetarium, architects sought technical help from Japan. The Planetarium is divided into four sections, allowing visitors to explore every unknown part. These four sections include the Nirmala Birla Art Gallery and Dinosaurium, Archaeology Centre, Window on Science, and Interactive science center.

There is a sky show conducted each day in the Planetarium, which is the most amazing feature. Another popular feature among the visitors is the Window on Science. The Birla Planetarium remains open every day except for Thursdays.

Address: Near Birla Temple, Ambedkar Colony, Hyderabad, Telangana, 500004, India

Timings: 11:30 am – 8:00 pm

Cost: Entry Fee: Rs.150/- | Combo (Planetarium + Science Museum): Rs.250/- | Combo (Planetarium + Science Museum + Art Gallery): Rs.325/-

Salar Jung Museum

The Salar Jung Museum is situated at the banks of the Musi River, with a collection of 47,000 printed books, 9,000 manuscripts, and 43,000 art objects. This collection is renowned for being the biggest collection of a single person in the world.

On the museum's first floor, there are 20 galleries alone, and the second floor has 18 galleries. The artifacts held in this museum have been significantly collected by Nawab Turba Ali Khan (Salar Jung I) and Mir Yousuf Ali Khan (Salar Jung III), along with the other descendants.

Address: Salar Jung Rd, near Minar Function Hall, Darulshifa, Hyderabad, Telangana 500002

Timings: 10am–5pm | Friday Closed

Cost: Adult Entry Fee: 10.00 per person, Child Entry Fee: 5.00 per person, Foreign Nationals Fee: 150 per person

Tank Bund

Tank Bund has a breathtaking view of the lake and a statue of Lord Buddha in the middle connecting both Secunderabad and Hyderabad. A popular hangout in the city, there are many other bronze statues of popular figures such as Nannaya, Sri Krishna Devaraya, Erra Pragada, Tikkanen, etc.

Near the Tank Bund, are other places such as Lumbini Park, the NTR memorial, Hazrat Saidani Ma Saheba tomb, Sanjeevaiah Park, the Secretariat building, etc. Among visitors, its appeal increases due to its location surrounded by other attractive Hyderabad tourist places.

Address: Tank Bund Road, Hyderabad, Telangana, 500004, India

Timings: 8:00 A.M To 10:00 P.M

Cost: Free

Purani Haveli

Located near Dewan Devdi, at the South East of Afzal Gunj Bridge, the Purani Haveli was built in the European style of architecture. Paired with centuries of rich history, this Haveli is an amalgamation of the 18th-century European style and traditional Indian courtyards.

The Haveli has beautiful antique furniture, designed interiors, massive courtyards, and rooms with mosaic floors and colorful tiled walls. There is also a library, Nizam's museum with varied collections, and Mukarram Jah Technical Institute inside the complex. Purani Haveli is open every day for visitors except for Fridays, and its tickets vary between children and adults.

Address: JUBILEE (HEAD OFFICE), CHARMINAR, PURANI HAVELI, , , HYDERABAD, TELANGANA, India (IN), Pin Code:- 500002

Timings: 10:30 AM - 5:00 PM, Closed on Friday

Cost: Adult: INR 80, Child: INR 15, Camera: INR 150

Durgam Cheruvu Park

Located near the Hi-Tech city, the Durgam Cheruvu is between many granite rocks and is also referred to as the Secret Lake by many people. It has another name, Madhapur Lake. It has been surrounded by rocks and spread over an astonishing 63 acres. The lake has been estimated to be around 2500 million years old. Among locals, it is considered that the water of this lake would have been used as drinking water in the previous centuries at Golconda Fort.

You can participate in various activities at the lake, such as pedal boating, trekking, and camping. There is also a cafeteria beside the lake that you can visit.

Address: Road Number 46, Masthan Nagar, CBI Colony, Jubilee Hills, Hyderabad, Telangana, 500033, India

Timings: 10:00 am – 6:30 pm

Cost: Free

Chilkur Balaji Temple

As its name suggests, this Balaji temple is located in a village named Chilkur, which is 30 km from Hyderabad. Located on the banks of Osman Sagar Lake, another name for this temple is also Visa Balaji temple.

This temple is dedicated to Lord Balaji Venkateswara with Bhoodevi and Sridevi. It is considered to be a 500-year-old temple. Many legends are associated with this temple, which attracts devotees and visitors daily.

Address: Chilkur, Chilkur Balaji temple road, Himayat Nagar, Hyderabad- 500075

Timings: 5:00 am to 8:00 pm

Cost: Free

Taj Falaknuma Palace

Located near Charminar, on a foothill, the Falaknuma palace is spread around 32 acres and made of marble. The palace has approximately 220 rooms and 22 halls, captivating chandeliers, a library, and many features that make it a historical marvel.

Address: Engine Bowli, Falaknuma, Hyderabad, Telangana 500053

Timings: 4:00 pm – 5:30 pm [Saturday and Sunday Only]
