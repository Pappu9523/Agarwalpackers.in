Navigating International Trade: The Power of Cargo Shipping

International trade has become a source of huge revenues for nations worldwide in today's global economic scenario. It is a source that fuels growth and creates opportunities for different companies across the globe. From small-scale to large-scale companies, businesses of all sizes rely on transporting goods across borders to reach out to new markets and meet consumer demands.

At the centre of the global trade of goods lies a solid and vital force known as cargo shipping. With many ships sailing the ocean with goods, Cargo shipping is the backbone of international trade that drives economic growth and innovation. It enables transporting goods and connecting companies to different markets around the world.

In this blog, we will explore international trade and its benefits, analyse cargo shipping services and its processes and reveal the role of international packers and movers in facilitating international trade through efficient cargo shipping. Read On!!!

What is International Trade?

International trade is a process that comprises the authorised trading and exchange of goods across borders. It is also known as the purchase and sale of goods and services by various businesses in different countries. Examples of goods bought and sold at the international marketplace include; raw materials, consumer goods, machinery and foods.

There are three types of international trade. They are:

  • Export Trade: Export Trade is the selling and delivering of goods manufactured and fabricated in one's home country to the citizens of another country. In this trade, the seller of the goods or services is known as the exporter.
  • Import Trade: Import trade is buying goods or services manufactured in another country to one's home country. In this trade, the buyer of the goods and services is known as the importer.
  • Entrepot Trade: Entrepot Trade is the process where goods are imported into a country and then re-exported without being distributed within the importing country.

Benefits of International Trade

These are the top benefits of foreign trade for both companies and the country involved in the trade -

  1. Expanding Target Markets & Increasing Revenues

    2. A larger target market due to international trade allows businesses to carry out production without fearing overproduction. This is because, with the target market's expansion, any excess products produced can be exported and sold internationally. This results in revenue growth for the company.

  2. Increased Job Opportunities

    3. Beyond the job opportunities in international trade, the industry helps generate jobs as companies expand their target market. As a company’s global market grows and market share increases, new job opportunities are also created.

  3. Better International Relations

    4. The economic relationship between countries that emerges with the help of international trade can lead to solid connections of alliances in other areas as well. When two countries engage in a significant amount of trade and business, they are more likely to reduce areas of conflict between them. This encourages peaceful relationships between these nations because they rely on one another for business and trade.

What is Cargo Shipping?

Cargo Shipping is also called "freight shipping." It is the movement of goods, merchandise, or commodities from one location to another using various modes of transportation, primarily ships or vessels. Cargo shipping services involve transporting large quantities of goods over long distances, primarily across national or international borders. There are various modes of cargo shipping. They are

  • Sea freight: Sea freight is the primary mode of cargo shipping for international trade. It is the best shipping mode for large cargo quantities and is the only option for large shipments because it does not fit into an aeroplane. The giant ocean container ships can load more than 20,000 containers. Sea freight offers the lowest cost per unit on a load compared to other freight types. However, it also has the longest transit time in most cases.
  • Air freight: Air freight is another cargo shipping mode for goods that require quick delivery and little inventory.
  • Road freight: Road freight is a regular mode of transport for international deliveries. Also, for global air, rail and ocean deliveries, the pick-up from the place of packing and loading by international packers to the air- or seaport is done by road transport. The same also applies to the final delivery to the consignee location.
  • Rail freight: Even though the global rail freight rate has reduced, rail freight container deliveries from countries like China to European countries have rapidly increased. Regarding miles hauled per unit of energy consumed, rail transport is one of the efficient modes of cargo shipping.

Processes Involved in Cargo Shipping

There are different processes involved in cargo shipping. An expert international packers and movers company follows these processes to ensure goods' safe and efficient conveyance from one country to another. They include -

  1. Checking restrictions

    Most countries have restrictions, laws and regulations regarding importing and exporting goods. These laws vary significantly, and ensuring that every shipment adheres to these rules is essential. Also, international cargo shipping involves passing through customs checkpoints. Customs authorities enforce these regulations, collect duties and taxes, and conduct inspections.

  2. Make bookings

    The process begins with the shipper(exporter) reserving a space on a cargo vessel. This involves contacting a shipping company or a freight forwarder to book a space for the cargo.

  3. Prepare Cargo

    The shipment must be adequately, securely packed, and labelled to ensure its safety during transportation. Packaging requirements usually vary depending on the type of goods being shipped. That is why one should hire experts from an international packer and move forward with this process, as they are well-versed in packing and loading goods. Also, the labels should include data such as the consignee's address, port of discharge and handling instructions.

  4. Document and complete paperwork

    The shipper must prepare and submit different documents, including a bill of lading (contract of carriage), commercial invoice, packing list, and the necessary permits or licences. These documents provide essential information about the cargo, its value, and its destination. Examples are -

    • Documentary Evidence of the Contract of Carriage
    • Proof of receipt of the goods for shipping
    • Cargo bill
    • Guidelines for handling, dispatching and delivering the consignment
  5. Drop cargo

    Once the physical cargo, documentation and paperwork have been prepared, they will be dropped off at the drop-off location. It is important to note that most shipping services companies publish their cargo drop-off time.

  6. Track Cargo

    The cargo vessel or flight will leave the port of origin and start its journey to the destination port. During this time, the shipper can monitor and track the shipment's progress using various Consignment Tracking Systems offered by the shipping company. This will allow them to monitor its location and estimated arrival time.

  7. Collecting Cargo

    After arrival, The shipment is delivered to the consignee (the party receiving the goods) at the designated location. Most times, distribution and further handling of the cargo is the responsibility of the consignee or other parties involved in the supply chain.

The Roles of International Packers and Movers in Facilitating International Trade Through Efficient Cargo Shipping

International packers and movers facilitate international trade through efficient cargo shipping. Here are some key roles they play:

  1. Packaging and Handling: International packers and movers are proficient in quality packaging goods for international shipment. They ensure that goods are adequately packed with suitable materials to resist damage during transportation. Also, they pack goods in a way that complies with international shipping regulations.

  2. Customs Adherence: International trade involves complicated customs rules and regulations. International Packers and movers are well trained in customs procedures like; documentation, including

  3. Handling of Logistics: Packers and movers manage the logistics aspects of international cargo shipping. They have experience with different stakeholders. Stakeholders include shipping lines, airlines, trucking companies, and customs authorities. When you rely on professional international packers and movers, you can rest easy knowing that they have everything under control. They take care of the entire process, from arranging the shipment to transportation and even tracking it along the way. With their expertise, they optimise routes to minimise both the transit time and cost, ensuring smooth and efficient transportation of goods.

  4. Freight Forwarding: International packers and movers also function as freight forwarders. They converge shipments from different sources into full container loads or arrange groupage shipments. They leverage their network and proficiency to find the cheapest yet most efficient shipping options. By consolidating shipments, they can provide competitive rates and adequate utilisation of transportation resources.

  5. Risk Management: Packers and movers help reduce risks connected with international cargo shipping. They offer insurance options to protect goods against damage or loss during transportation. Furthermore, they use best practices in securing cargo to help reduce the chances of theft, breakage, or other risks.

  6. Storage: International packers and movers often retain warehouse and storage facilities at important moving points. They provide temporary storage solutions for goods that need short-term holding before being transported. This is useful, especially when there are delays or disruptions in shipping schedules. This provides flexibility and reduces the impact on trade flows.
