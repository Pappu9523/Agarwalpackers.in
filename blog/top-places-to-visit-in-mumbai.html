Top 10+ Places To Visit In Mumbai

Everyone can be impressed by a trip to Mumbai, a historic city that never ceases to brim with vitality, vigour, and enthusiasm. A major tourist destination in Maharashtra, Mumbai is also one of the world's busiest cities. You may have your fair share of adventures in various areas of interest, yet just getting used to the environment here is an experience in and of itself.

There is something for everyone here, from museums and galleries to shopping malls and nightclubs. There are also plenty of natural picnic areas and historical landmarks to explore. Following are the top 10 most attractive places to visit in Mumbai.

List of Top Mumbai Tourist Places

Top 10 places to visit in Mumbai are:

  • Gateway of India
  • Gorai Beach
  • Elephanta Caves
  • Taraporewala Aquarium
  • Chhatrapati Shivaji Terminus
  • Manori Beach
  • Mahakali Caves
  • Haji Ali Shrine
  • Marine Drive
  • Sanjay Gandhi National Park
  • Siddhivinayak Temple
  • Powai Lake
  • Global Vipassana Pagoda
  • Basilica of Our Lady of the Mount
  • Hanging Gardens
  • Juhu Chowpatty Beach

Briefing of Mumbai Famous Places with Name

Gateway of India

Although located in Mumbai, the Gateway to India symbolises the whole of India. The entrance is situated in the city's southern region and looks out into the magnificent Arabian Sea. This majestic structure was built in tribute to King George V and Queen Mary's 1911 trip to India. It took George Wittet almost 13 years to finish this project. On December 4, 1924, the monument opened formally.

Symbolising the harmony between Hinduism and Islam, the Gateway of India includes elements from both architectural traditions. It is a well-known landmark and among the top beautiful places to visit in Mumbai.

Address: Kartavya Path, India Gate, New Delhi, Delhi 110001

Timings: Open for 24 hours

Cost: Free

Gorai Beach

The communities of Uttan, Manori, Dongri, and Marve surround the popular campsite of Gorai beach. Besides, Essel World and The Global Vipassana Pagoda are within walking distance. Given its proximity to Bhayandar, Borivali, and Malad, this beach is a favourite among couples and families.

If you're searching for a relaxing weekend escape from the hectic pace of Mumbai, Gorai is a great option. It's one of the city's cleanest and safest beaches.

Timings: 24 hrs

Cost: Free

Elephanta Caves

Elephanta Caves, located on Elephanta Island, take about a 40-minute boat ride from the Gateway of India. Designated as a UNESCO World Heritage Site, the Elephanta Caves have been around since 600 CE. The Portuguese utilised Hindu and Buddhist deity statues made of stone as targets for their swordplay, causing the sculptures to suffer extensive damage.

Elephanta Caves is one of India's seven wonders for a reason—the sculptures' artwork is a testament to the high level of creative achievement of the time.

Address: Elephanta Caves Mumbai Address: Gharapuri, Maharashtra, 400094, India

Timings: 9:00 AM to 5:00 PM (Monday Closed)

Cost: INR 40/- Per Person

Taraporewala Aquarium

The aquarium is among the oldest in India and the best place to visit in Mumbai. Tourists can view both freshwater and saltwater fish here. It features a 12-foot-long glass tunnel with a 360-degree view. Furthermore, a special pool is designed only for kids, who can safely interact with fish by touching and petting them.

Over 2,000 fish, representing more than 400 species, swim freely in Taraporewala Aquarium.

Address: Netaji Subhash Chandra Bose Road, Marine Drive, Near Charni Road Railway Station, Girgaon, Mumbai, Maharashtra, 400002, India

Timings: 10:00 AM - 8:00 PM

Cost: INR 60/- Per Person

Chhatrapati Shivaji Terminus

Previously known as Victoria Terminus, the structure got completed in 1897. The terminal was designated a World Heritage Site by UNESCO in 2004. It is an example of numerous architectural styles, including traditional Indian, Victorian, and Gothic styles. Although it is a terminal where trains arrive and depart, one cannot help but be in awe of the magnificent design of the structure. This terminus is a Mumbai must-see location, regardless of whether you plan to ride a train.

Address: Chhatrapati Shivaji Terminus Area, Fort, Mumbai, Maharashtra, 400001, India

Timings: 12:00 AM - 12:00 AM

Cost: Free

Manori Beach

Near the Mumbai suburbs lies the serene village of Manori. It is commonly called Manori Creek. Local Kolis, Kunbis, and Bhandari peasants who mostly fish and cultivate in Manori are often known as East Indians.

Manori, sometimes known as Mini Goa, is a popular weekend destination for Mumbai residents since it's quite accessible and offers you the feeling that you're in Goa.

Address: Malad West, Maharashtra, 400095, India

Timings: 7:00 am – 08:00 pm

Cost: Free

Mahakali Caves

Address: There are various clusters of old rock-cut temples hidden in Mumbai's suburbs. Ancient Kondivita Caves, subsequently known as Mahakali Caves, are one such landmark. The Mahakali caves are a group of 19 rock-cut caverns that date from between the first and sixth centuries AD. They are carved out of black basalt.

There are seven images of Buddha and characters from Buddhist mythology in the biggest cave (cave 9). You can find this Buddhist Shrine in Andheri's eastern suburb. It is situated close to the intersection of Seepz and the Jogeshwari-Vikhroli connecting road. The route connecting the landmark to Andheri Kurla Road is Mahakali Caves Road. You can spend a day and enjoy ancient handwork in Mahakali Caves, one of the best places to visit in Mumbai with friends.

Address: Mahakali Caves, Sunder Nagar, Andheri East, Mumbai

Timings: 9:00 AM - 6:00 PM

Cost: INR 20/- Per Person

Haji Ali Shrine

Haji Ali Shrine, situated in Worli, is an example of Indian Muslim architecture and is among the stunning places to visit in Mumbai. The 85-foot-tall Shrine is situated on a plot of land 4500 metres long. The Shrine's pillars are beautifully crafted, and the structure's mirror work is breathtaking. People of all faiths and beliefs are attracted to the Shrine due to its eye-soothing architecture.

Address: Dargah Rd, Haji Ali, Mumbai, Maharashtra, 400026, India

Timings: 5:30 am – 10:00 pm

Cost: Free

Marine Drive

Marine Drive is conveniently located in the middle of the city, making it accessible from any location in Mumbai. It connects a 3-kilometre-long bay with the Arabian Sea. The length connects Nariman Point with Babulnath in a 'C' form.

The location provides an amazing sunset view. Tourists are drawn to Marine Drive because they may stroll along the sidewalk and enjoy the refreshing wind there. The spectacular drive alone is enough to place this location on the list of the best places to visit in Mumbai for couples.

Address: Mumbai, Maharashtra, 400004, India

Timings: 12:00 am – 12:00 pm

Cost: Free

Sanjay Gandhi National Park

Spread out across 104 square kilometres, Sanjay Gandhi National Park is one of the most popular national parks in Asia and, as such, earns a spot among the best places to visit in Mumbai rankings. This park can amuse families with its wide variety of entertaining activities. You may spend a wonderful day boating on the park's artificial lake and seeing the park's big cats from a safari cage.

Take a walk through the woods to reach the Gandhi Tekdi, a monument dedicated to Mahatma Gandhi. Another well-liked attraction in this area, particularly among children, is the toy train, Van Rani.

You may also visit the 2000-year-old Kanheri Caves to view more Buddha artefacts. A trip to the forest's Jain Temple and its unspoiled nature pathways will also leave a lasting impression.

Address: Borivali East, Mumbai, Maharashtra, 400066, India

Timings: 7:30 am – 6:30 pm (Monday Closed)

Cost: INR 58/- Per Person

Siddhivinayak Temple

The Siddhivinayak Temple, devoted to the Hindu god Ganesh, is one of the most popular places to visit in Mumbai for devotees. Worshippers from all over India visit the temple to ask Siddhivinayak Ganapati for his blessings, including well-known actors from Bollywood and athletes.

This one-of-a-kind statue of Lord Ganesha is hewn from a single block of black stone and has his trademark trunk curved to the right. Plan a trip with your family to this temple for a spiritually enlightening experience.

Address: S.K.Bole Marg, Prabhadevi, Prabhadevi, Mumbai, Maharashtra, 400028, India

Timings: 5:30 am – 9:50 pm

Cost: Free

Powai Lake

The beautiful artificial Powai Lake has a depth of 12 metres and an expanse of 6.6 kilometres. Located east of the lake, the IIT Bombay is a dazzling addition to the area. Stunning pink-purple Balsam plants and a wide variety of species, including bulbuls, sunbirds, kingfishers, kites, storks, herons, ducks, parakeets, and doves, draw nature lovers to this lake.

Address: Powai, Maharashtra, 400076, India

Timings: 12:00 am – 12:00 am

Cost: Free

Global Vipassana Pagoda

Global Vipassana Pagoda is a medical building designed to symbolise harmony and tranquillity. The lectures here aim to dispel misunderstandings about Vipassana and Lord Buddha's teachings. The pagoda design is a statement of appreciation to Burma for maintaining the Vipassana meditation tradition. The building's central dome is the biggest in the world, with no supporting pillars.

Address: Near Essel World | Next to Esselworld, Gorai Village, Maharashtra, 400091, India

Timings: 9:00 am – 7:00 pm

Cost: Free

Basilica of Our Lady of the Mount

Historically, the 16th century is when the Basilica of Our Lady of the Mount first appeared. Two sculptures of the Blessed Virgin Mary in this Roman Catholic church are rather well-known, and both have fascinating backstories.

Every year, on the first Sunday following September 8, a festive celebration honouring the birth of the Virgin Mary is hosted, and the renowned Bandra fair follows. This church is frequented by many Christians and Hindus, making it into the rankings of the best places to visit in Mumbai.

Address: Basilica of Our Lady of the Mount,, Bandra West, Mount Mary, Bandra West, Mumbai, Maharashtra, 400050, India

Timings: 8:00 am – 1:00 pm and 2:00 pm – 8:30 pm

Cost: Free

Hanging Gardens

Every individual needs a break from their routines every once in a while. Bring your family to this magnificent Hanging Garden to relax and let your hair down among the flowerbeds, magnificent water fountains, and lush green hedges in the form of animals.

The distinctive characteristics of this park are the multicoloured flower clock in the middle and the various shrubs in the form of animals. The Hanging Gardens provide breathtaking views of the Arabian Sea as well.

Another intriguing element that will make your visit unforgettable is The Boot House or Old Woman's Shoe, entirely made of stone.

Address: Simla Nagar, Malabar Hill, Mumbai, Maharashtra, 400006, India

Timings: 5:00 am – 9:00 pm

Cost: Free

Juhu Chowpatty Beach

Juhu Beach is one of the most popular and well-known unique places to visit in Mumbai. Its coastline is expansive. People may be seen running on the beach in the early morning and late evening.

The evening is the ideal time to visit this beach because of the wonderful lighting, the sunset, and the abundance of food and other goods available from street sellers.

Address: Juhu Tara Road, Juhu, Mumbai, Maharashtra, 400049, India

Timings: 12:00 am – 12:00 am

Cost: Free

Conclusion

As this city has much to offer, there are many more places to visit in Mumbai and explore. If you have the opportunity, check out some of Mumbai's notable museums and other historical landmarks. The city's natural beauty is only a short trip away from the nearby hill stations.
