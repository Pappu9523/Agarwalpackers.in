Moving to the Silicon Valley of India

Bangalore is located in the southeast of the state of Karnataka. Often known as "Garden City" and "Silicon Valley of India. It lies in the center of the Mysore Plateau. The government of Karnataka accepted and enforced a proposal by U.R.Ananthamurthy to change the city's name to Bengaluru from 1st November 2006. The Bangalore region was ruled by several dynasties before the British Raj in the 19th century when it served as the British Administration's headquarters for a brief period. The inhibition of modern Bangalore took place in the 1950s as the government boosted its investment in public sector undertakings. With the national reforms of 1991, this city's course changed, and it emerged as the nation's IT hub. Due to the large number of IT companies located here, the city contributed 33% of India'sIndia's total IT exports. The headquarters of several public sector undertakings such as Bharat Electronics Ltd, Hindustan Aeronautics Limited, ISRO, and many more are located here.
